# Iris

Your second brain on an infinite canvas.

![Canvas overview](docs/screenshots/canvas-overview.png)

---

Iris is where you put things you don't want to lose. Tweets, articles, ideas, images, notes — dropped onto a canvas you can freely arrange. No folders, no tags, no hierarchy. Just space.

---

## The canvas

Everything lives on an infinite 2D surface. You pan around it, zoom in and out, and place things wherever they feel right. Cards can be anything — a link you saved, a tweet that made you think, an image, a note you wrote. You can have multiple canvases for different contexts.

The canvas stays out of your way. Drag a URL in and it becomes a card with a preview. Drag an image in and it sits there. Nothing forces you to organize it a certain way.

![Canvas with content arranged](docs/screenshots/canvas-worklog.png)

---

## Cards

Drop any link onto the canvas and Iris fetches a preview — title, description, thumbnail. Twitter/X links render as the actual tweet. Images stay as images. Notes get a rich text editor.

You can resize cards, reorder them, move them around. If you're building a reading list, a mood board, or just collecting references for something you're working on — it all goes here.

![Visual cards and images](docs/screenshots/canvas-images.png)

---

## Search

`⌘K` opens a search palette that reaches across everything — cards, notes, links — on all your canvases.

![Search](docs/screenshots/canvas-search.png)

---

## Notes and work log

Iris has a work log built in. Start a session when you sit down to work, stop it when you're done. Each session has a timer and a note attached to it — a scratchpad for what you figured out, what you're stuck on, what to pick up next.

The note panel slides in alongside the canvas so you never lose your place. When you open the same task again later, your notes from past sessions show up below.

![Session notes](docs/screenshots/notes.png)

---

## Works on mobile too

On mobile, your canvas content becomes a list. Same cards, same data — just a layout that fits the screen.

---

Iris is a personal tool. It's not trying to be a team workspace or a project manager. It's a place for your own thinking.
